**LemmaHead: Boosting Mathematical Proof Generation with Retrieval-Augmented LLMs**

Large Language Models have shown remarkable capabilities across various domains, but tackling complex mathematical reasoning and generating formal proofs in languages like Lean remains a significant hurdle. Traditional approaches often rely on fine-tuning models on vast amounts of mathematical text, hoping they implicitly learn the underlying logic and structure. However, this approach often falls short when faced with the rigorous demands of formal proof generation.

The challenge lies not just in understanding mathematical concepts, but in constructing step-by-step logical arguments that are formally verifiable. LLMs can struggle with maintaining long chains of reasoning and accessing precise, authoritative mathematical knowledge exactly when needed.

This paper introduces LemmaHead, a novel approach that enhances LLMs' formal proof generation capabilities by integrating Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). The core idea is to provide the LLM with access to a curated knowledge base derived from authoritative mathematical textbooks, specifically those used in Olympiad-level competition preparation. This structured access aims to supply the model with relevant definitions, theorems, examples, and problem-solving strategies to ground its reasoning.

LemmaHead's knowledge base is constructed by processing mathematical textbooks. A key challenge was accurately digitizing content containing complex mathematical expressions and symbols. The authors employed a vision-based pipeline to capture page images and transcribe them into LaTeX, preserving fidelity. This LaTeX content was then carefully chunked, keeping related information like problems and their solutions together, to create contextually rich units for retrieval.

The researchers experimented with GPT-4 as the base model and explored three different RAG-assisted pipelines:

1.  **Basic RAG:** A simple query based on the problem statement retrieves relevant context from LemmaHead, which is then prepended to the prompt for GPT-4 to generate the proof.
2.  **Enhanced Query Generation (EQG):** Instead of a simple query, GPT-4 first generates an "enhanced query" to LemmaHead based on the problem, aiming for more targeted retrieval.
3.  **Iterative Proof Augmentation (IPA):** This is a multi-step process. GPT-4 generates an initial informal proof. This proof, along with the original problem, is used to query LemmaHead for relevant context. GPT-4 then generates an improved informal proof based on this retrieved context. This process can be iterated multiple times. Finally, the refined informal proof is used to guide the generation of the formal Lean proof.

These pipelines were evaluated on the MiniF2F dataset, a benchmark of formal Olympiad-level mathematics problems. The primary metric was Pass@1 rate – the percentage of problems for which the model generated a correct formal Lean proof on the first attempt.

The results showed that RAG-assisted approaches, particularly RAG-assisted GPT-4 with IPA, significantly outperformed the baseline GPT-4 and even other state-of-the-art methods like Human-guided GPT-4 and GPT-f (a fine-tuned GPT-3 model). Interestingly, the basic RAG pipeline, without EQG or IPA, performed worse than the baseline GPT-4, suggesting that poorly retrieved or irrelevant context can actually be detrimental.

The IPA approach, even with a limited number of iterations tested due to computational constraints, showed the most promising results, highlighting the value of iterative refinement guided by external knowledge.

While LemmaHead demonstrates the significant potential of RAG for boosting LLM performance in complex reasoning tasks like formal proof generation, challenges remain. The computational cost of iterative methods like IPA increases with the number of iterations, and LLMs still struggle with generating very long or deeply nested proofs. Future work could explore more efficient IPA strategies, test a higher number of iterations, and investigate integrating LemmaHead with other LLMs beyond GPT-4.
